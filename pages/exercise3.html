Exhausting a Crowd - Kyle McDonald


Exhausting a Crowd is an online crowdsourced art that allows users to annotate anything in the pre-recorded 12 hours footage recorded by the street surveillance camera from cities around the world.


My first 10 minutes on the website had me enthralled with amusement. I couldn't help but audibly laugh when a user-generated text box stating “make sure you get my good angle” appeared over the head of someone posing next to a statue for a photo her friend was taking. Even ones that didn’t make sense, such as a user tagging an interestingly shaped streetlight as “a public mind control device” felt like premium was perceived by my brain as premium entertainment.


It was only when I read an article about it that I understood the artist’s intention behind the project. A topic much more serious than what my reaction led me to believe; the normalization of public surveillance. We can all probably think of moments in our week that we would prefer not to have annotated by a 14-year teenager from across the globe.


But why was it that even though I’m enrolled in a university program which places so much importance on awareness and questioning of developing technologies, this idea did not even cross my mind? How could a project about an ethical issue become a global source of entertainment?
